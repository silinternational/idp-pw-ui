How do I change my password?

To change your password, simply go to Change password, login and enter a new password.

I forgot my password, how do I reset it?

There are several ways to reset your password. The main method sends an email with a reset link to your primary email address. If you do not have access to your primary email account you may also choose to have the link sent to you supervisor or spouse. The third option requires setting up recovery methods in advance; these methods can include additional email accounts or phone numbers which you have. It is recommended that you add at least one additional recovery method to your account to help ensure you can reset your password in a time of need. You can reset your password by going to Forgot password, entering your username or email address and proceeding through the process.

What is a recovery method?

Recovery methods are ways for you to verify your identity so that you can set a new password for your account. You are encouraged to set up either email or phone based recovery methods. Then, in the event of forgetting your password, you can use one of these recovery methods for resetting your password. You may either have a link sent by email or a reset code delivered as a text message or voice call to your phone. You can manage your recovery methods by going to My profile.

Password tips

Passwords are just like keys to your house and should be treated similarly. Below are some tips that will help you choose good passwords and keep your Insite account safe and secure.

  • Use a password manager. Password managers such as LastPass, KeePass, and 1Password all make it easy to generate highly complex passwords and save them for you so that you do not need to remember them all. A password manager makes it very easy to meet all the following recommendations.
  • Use unique passwords. Do not use the same password for more than one site. In the same way you do not use the same key for your house, car, office, and safe deposit box, you do not want to use the same password for multiple sites.
  • Use complex passwords that contain a mix of words, numbers, special characters, and both uppercase and lowercase letters. Do not make simple letter/number substitutions like a 3 for the letter E or ! for an I or L, these are common and easily cracked. Long random sentences like “Big smelly frog lives 12 years!” are easy to remember and hard to crack.
  • Don’t use family names or special dates like birthdays or anniversaries. This information is not always private and is so commonly used that hackers will use this information in their attempts to crack your password.
  • Never share your password with anyone!
